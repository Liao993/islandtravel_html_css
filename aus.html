Where in Australia?

I was lucky enough to visit the continent of Australia several times. I visited Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane there. Since Australia is surrounded by the ocean, they often refer to it as an island continent. It is full of immigrants, as Australia is one of the immigration destinations as USA and Canada. There, you find immigrant people from every race, and also they have the Aboriginal Australians who are the indigenous people of Australia. If you need a community from your home country that would be very easy to find in Australia, as there is the Lebanese community, Egyptian community, Korean community and so on and so forth. Everyone who ever visited Australia fell in love with it. There is a feeling of warmth and happiness that runs through the whole continent. It is always sunny in Australia, so I think that is one of the reasons why people are so happy there. Most of the people there are healthy, always working out, and tend to eat healthy food. Australia also is so green, so it is tempting to eat outside and do all the activities outdoors. The meat, fruits and vegetables are amazing in Australia, and they are so fresh. If you like steaks and excellent wine, then definitely Australia is the place for you.

Before going to Australia

One of the things people need to know before going to Australia is that they have strict rules that everyone who is landing and entering Australia has to follow. I remember we used to make a lot of passenger announcements in that flight that any animal or dairy products, or decorations that are made of wood, or shoes that have certain insoles on them, or anything that has a soil attached to it are strictly not allowed. There are serious fines and confiscations that happen in Australian airports, and they do a lot of random checks to search bags as well, and there are always the service dogs to sniff the bags on the baggage carousel.

The second thing to check before going there is the weather. The weather in Australia is opposite to the weather in so many destinations of the world. The weather is similar to the one in South Africa, both of those destinations have their Christmas during the summertime for example, where December there is a hot summer season. When it comes to Winter, August is considered the winter in Australia and South Africa for instance. So, before you plan your trip, and let’s say it falls in August, you need to pack with winter clothes, boots and jackets, whereas if your vacation falls in December, you need to carry with you shorts, swimsuits and flip flops. Therefore, being updated and knowledgeable about the weather in Australia is extremely important

Third advice is the extreme importance of carrying sunscreen protection, as the UV rays there are so harmful. Also, keep in mind that Australia has a lot of insects, bugs and different other creatures such as reptiles and snakes, so you need to take your precautions and get more updated about that, especially if you will do outdoor activities in nature.

Sydney

Sydney is a very popular destination, and it has a lot of immigrants, especially Lebanese. It is so vibrant and full of life, and has a lot of things to explore. If you are downtown, most of the attractions are within walking distance from each other. You can visit the famous Sydney Bridge, by either booking a tour to climb it, or just to see it from a distance where you could still capture some great photos. A visit to Sydney Opera House is definitely a must, as the opera house has a very unique structure, it is shaped like the mists of sailing boats. You can book a guided tour of the opera, and they have different packages for that. If you decide not to enter, you can walk by and capture some photos as well. Darling Harbour is a great location to have a nice dinner, especially if you would like to visit a steakhouse. After you finish the delicious steak, do not forget to try one of Australia’s famous desserts which is sticky date pudding.

Melbourne

Unlike Sydney that has a lot of Lebanese communities, Melbourne has a large Greek Community, as well as Italians. Melbourne is very dynamic, and the life rhythm there is so busy. Tourist attractions there have a different vibe than Sydney. They are more like street sightseeing, where you get to see a lot of old architecture, and many museums and street arts. Once you land in Melbourne, start the day so early and wear very comfortable walking shoes, as you will be doing a lot of walking there.

Start by seeing the Street Arts, where you find the Graffiti Art on the walls there so popular, and Melbourne is considered one of the cities that is very renowned for it. Pay a visit to the Botanic Gardens, where you can forget the urban city life for a bit, and relax in a wonderful green nature. A lot of people are doing their walk or run exercise over there. If you are fan of open food markets, then you must visit Queen Victoria Markets, where you can buy cheese, olives and sweets. Some stalls also sell handicrafts, and they often run interesting events there.

If you love shopping like me, then Melbourne is definitely the place for that. They have huge shoppings malls, and a lot of shopping streets that have varieties of international and Australian products, visit Chapel Street and Bridge street for a day of a shopping spree. Since there are a lot of Greek and Italian communities in Melbourne, you need to try the delicious Greek cuisine by going to the Greek District, where you get to try authentic Greek food prepared by Greeks themselves. If you are a fan of Italian food, then visit Lygon District where you can have espresso in the Italian cafes, and get to buy some Italian products, as well as indulge in the Italian authentic cuisine prepared by Italians there.

Brisbane

Brisbane is a very calm destination where life is so slow and relaxed in comparison to Sydney and Melbourne. It has the ambience of the countryside, and the people there are very nice. I was lucky that I spent the new year of 2009 there once with my colleagues from the flight. We started New Year’s Eve by having dinner all together, and then we started walking the streets of Brisbane downtown, waiting for the fireworks over the bridge at midnight. What drew my attention while walking is an area called South Bank Parklands on River Brisbane. They are located in the heart of the city, and they are free public swimming pools that form a big lagoon, on a man-made sandy beach. On New Year’s Eve, it was crowded with people, families and kids that were swimming there the whole night and early morning. After that, we headed and climbed the Story Bridge, where you eat to witness the countdown and watch the beautiful fireworks. It was also very packed and crowded with people, however the vibes of that night were really nice.

One of the places worth visiting in Brisbane is the Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary, where you see the wildlife, and get to see Australia's kangaroos and Koalas. They have packages there that have entrance fees with sessions to hold the Koalas, and take pictures and videos with them. One tip of advice is that if you are scared of animals or scared to hold koalas, do not choose the package that involves holding and carrying them. I was a bit nervous and the Koala that I held felt it, and then it got scared and refused to take pictures with other guests. The guards were very mad at me, and blamed me for scaring the koala, and they were totally right. I was just selfish and wanted to take pictures with it anyhow.

On another flight that I had to Brisbane, myself and one of my friends in the flight decided to visit the Gold Coast. We took the train from Brisbane for an hour and a half and arrived there. It was like a magical paradise, and the hotel we stayed there was even called: Surfers Paradise. The hotel is like a skyscraper with lots of rooms with breathtaking views of the ocean. There are a lot of restaurants, swimming suit shops, cafes and lots of surfing learning companies across the beach. It is very crowded during the high seasons, and the best thing to do is to go for night walks, and observe the moon over the ocean, especially if it is a full moon. There are lots of night markets at night, and most of the people are out of their hotels enjoying the place.

Islandness in Australia

Since Australia is a continental island, I could not relate the meanings of islandness to it at all. The only exception to that is my Gold Coast visit. Although Australia is geographically considered a continent island, I guess people who live there do not feel that they are living on an island either. To me, I consider it a huge mainland with the rhythm of a busy city. However at the Gold Coast, I felt that I was on a tropical island, the same way as I felt when I visited Boracay or Maldives. I felt like I was in a recreational resort on the Gold Coast, where I could do a lot of water sports, eat seafood, and sunbathe. For sure there are other aspects of Australia that I’m missing which might carry the meanings of islandness in them, or to people who explored Australia in more depth and spent more time there, where they could relate to that.

Despite not being able to find meanings and connections to the notion of islandness there, Australia is still one of my favorite destinations that I have been to. It has its own magical charm that can capture anyone’s heart. Some of the immigrants that I know who lived there for sometime love it deeply, and would like to continue the rest of their lives in Australia.
