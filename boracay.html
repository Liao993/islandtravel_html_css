Inspiration to go visit Boracay

I used to travel to Manila, the capital of the Philippines, a lot, and the flights used to be full of tourists. I had no idea that the Philippines had some of the most exotic islands in the world during my early flying years. My airline used to fly only to the capital city, Manila. I also have a lot of Filipino close friends, and one of them is my best friend Maria for many years until now. I started asking people back then to find out what is the secret behind visiting the Philippines, and why so many tourists go there. I then found out that it was because of the stunning islands there. I also used to see a lot of brochures and advertisements in Manila about those islands and wonderful pictures from them.

I heard the name Boracay quite often, as it was so popular. Then I decided that I have to go and visit a famous island there, and I chose to go to Boracay. My travel companion was Maria, my best friend, and we arranged and planned everything together. I still owe Maria a visit to Egypt because back then we agreed that she would show me her country, and I would show her Egypt afterwards. All this goes back to the year 2014, because after that Boracay was shut down for quite some time for redevelopment. Therefore, my experience in Boracay was based on the old days before its closure, so it might be completely different from people who visited it recently.

When to visit Boracay and what to bring with you?

Boracay is best visited from December to April, where it is most of the time sunny and perfect for water sports, and very less rain. It is a tropical island so it is always warm. Basically, all you need to bring with you is tops, shorts, bathing suits and flip flops. People are walking in these items day and night. There are also some shops there that sell all these things, in case you do not want to bring anything with you, and would rather buy everything from there. Of course, do not forget your sunscreen, tanning oil and mosquito spray as you would definitely need them, however, they sell them there too. Regarding bringing money, most of the hotels accept debit and credit card payments, however, best advice is to always have some currency exchange to Philippine pesos. You can also bring some US dollars if finding pesos would be hard to find in your country. If you live in the Gulf region, it would be so easy to do that before you head to the Philippines, as there are many Filipino expats in that region of the world. I also recommend carrying cash whenever you go to restaurants or do some water activities for tipping, as tipping is very popular there. You can pay everything in cards, however, people would expect you to tip them in cash as a courtesy.

The Journey to Boracay

In Abu Dhabi, there are direct flights to the capital Manila which are 9 hours long. We booked with Philippine Airlines full fare tickets as we wanted to feel like tourists, and we did not fly with our airline in order not to feel like we were on a duty flight. We had a transit in Manila for like 4 hours waiting for our connecting domestic flight that would take us to Boracay. The flight to Boracay was very short of only 1 hour, and that was through taking a propeller, a small aircraft that flies at a very low altitude, and it was quite fun. After arrival in Boracay, we had to take a ferry that would drop us on the port where our hotel was located. Basically, at that time, Boracay consisted of 3 stations or ports where the ferry goes to from Boracay airport, according to where people have booked their hotels. Boracay had station 1, station two and station 3. They are all on the same beach, however each one of them has different standards of hotels and services. They are all accessible through walking on the island, for example: you can walk from station 1 to station 3 by foot, and vice versa. As I said earlier, I do not know how things are different now from the year I visited it back in 2014.

Things to do at Boracay

What surprised me when the ferry dropped us at our station was that there are no cars there, so basically you have to walk around most of the time, and use the ferry if you would like to get away to some other spots, or go back to the airport. I was a bit shocked, because we had to drag our suitcases on the sand all the way to the hotel we booked there, and the wheels on my suitcase had sand trapped in them which annoyed me a little bit. However, once we arrived in the hotel and put everything in the rooms, I totally forgot about that and we began exploring the place.

The thing you will do the most is walking around and jumping from one restaurant or bar to another if you decide not to swim on any day for example. You can also relax by the beach especially during sunset. Sunset by the beach in Boracay is one of the best things you would ever see. Boracay has a lot of sailing boats which you can also take a ride in one of them, or you can just chill by the beach and watch a large number of them sailing on the water under the sunset rays.

Images: Sunset, Beach and Massage

There are a lot of street vendors roaming around people who sell beach accessories, swimming suits, coconut drinking water, and fresh mangoes. Some of them also belong to tourism companies and offer to give you some water activity packages with cheaper prices than those at the hotels. You can also do body massage or manicure and pedicure, and they do that on the beach loungers, and you get to bargain there on basically everything outside the hotels. If they like you and you are nice to them, they will reduce the price very low.

In regards to mangoes, they are very popular all over the Philippines, and they are very famous for it. You can even get dried mangoes in some shops. However, on the beach you have to try the fresh cut mangoes the vendors sell, and they offer you either yellow or green ones depending on your preference, and they offer you salt or fish sauce with it. This is the traditional Filipino way of eating fresh mangoes.

Images: Coconut Water, Fresh Mango and Island Seafood

Filipino cuisine is so popular with so many varieties of local authentic dishes at the restaurants, and there are also so many grocery stores and bakery shops all around the island where you can buy snacks if you do not like the food there. Restaurants there also offer international dishes such as pizza, burgers and fries as well. I have only tried two local things which is chicken adobo, and a local pastry called pan de coco, and both are very mouthwatering. In the grocery shops, they have those crackers which are individually packed and called: Skyflakes, you can just throw them in your bag and carry them around with you in case of any sudden hunger pangs during your beach time activities.

When you decide to do some water activities, you can take some of the packages they offer you at the beach for reasonable prices, or you can always opt for the option of packages from the hotels which tend to be overpriced. The most famous water sports there are taking a banana boat ride, flying fish, parasailing, diving, snorkeling and taking a ride on a sailing boatb. As I mentioned earlier, these activities are very strenuous and dangerous, and you basically get to do them at your own risk. Therefore, if someone is suffering from any medical illness, disability or unfit, I would not recommend that at all. Also, do not forget to put sunscreen on if you are too sensitive to the sun. I remember I was so tanned, almost burned towards the end of my trip.

Images: Banana Boat, Boat Ride, and Snorkeling

At night time, a lot of people are socializing in bars, clubs and restaurants, or you can sit by the beach again and enjoy the fire shows they do there, they are very popular and in high demand over there. Once it is sunset, please do not forget to put mosquito spray all over your body, as there are a lot of mosquitos over there and it would be very uncomfortable to be bitten by them all night. We also bought a bottle of room mosquito spray as they tend to enter hotel rooms as well, we used to spray the room when we get back from our day activities and before we head out to explore the night life on the island.

Souvenirs and Gifts to buy from Boracay

The most iconic gift or souvenir you can get to yourself or to someone from there is Puka Shell necklaces and bracelets. They believe that puka shells provide good luck, and are also a form of protection for sailors in the old days if they wore them. The other thing that drew my attention at souvenir shops are Capiz Shell plates and trays. You also cannot leave Boracay without buying a pearl set of earrings which they are quite well known for, and you can get them in many colors. If some of your friends like edible gifts, you buy them dried mangoes and they will really thank you for that as they are very delicious.

Islandness in Boracay

Boracay is a typical example of a sun, sand and sea island. It is full of palm coconut trees, warm tropical climate, white sandy beaches and interesting marine life. The people there are extremely nice and they welcome and take care of the tourists really well. Local people are proud of their culture, food, and selling their man made products. You will be able to recognize tourists from local people as local people are so tanned and sun kissed from being exposed to the sun everyday.

The dominant vibes there are vibes of happiness and excitement, as everybody is on holidays enjoying their time. Feelings of separation, isolation and being on a periphery do not exist there, as people are busy enjoying their time, and even if they decide to leave this island and go to another one, it is still like paradise all over the place. Heading back to capital city Manila is awesome, and full of fun things to do. However, I would still believe that if Boracay is being visited in the wrong season by mistake, people could experience rainy weather conditions, some flight cancellations or ferry delays which would have an unpleasant impact on them for sure, unlike when visited in the recommended months of the year.
