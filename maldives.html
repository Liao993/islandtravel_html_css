Getting a Glimpse of Maldives before Being There

When I started flying, we used to operate Maldives flights as turnaround flights, initially we did not have any layovers there. I have always heard about Maldives and its connection to honeymooners. I was always hearing or reading that people go and spend their honeymoon there, and that Maldives is like paradise, and that it is also very expensive. When they scheduled me for the turnaround flight, I was kind of excited because I would get to see the profile of the passengers going there, and also, I would also see the islands from the skies. However, I was also frustrated due to the fact that we would go back to Abu Dhabi and not have a layover in that magical place.

The passenger profile mainly consisted of honeymooners from the gulf region, especially local Emirati newlyweds. They are normally wealthy and most of them were seated in premium cabins, i.e. first and business class cabins. The flight to Maldives from Abu Dhabi is short, around 4 hours going there, and 4 hours coming back. I used to ask the passengers sometimes about their plans in Maldives and I got to know that it is a quiet and serene place to be. I also found out that the population of Maldives are mainly muslims, so it was more convenient for the Arab tourists to go there as the food would be Halal, and the culture of the local people would be kind of similar. Passengers used to tell me about the resorts they booked their trips for, and how many days they planned to spend there, and the activities they would be doing. Most of the resorts that the passengers booked at were top notch resorts, and they booked an average of a full week there. Their resorts would consist of small private individual villas with their own swimming pool, and a private beach.

In regards to the view from the skies, it was marvelous. I was speechless when I first saw the view from the top, and usually the captain of the flight would make an announcement so that passengers would look out of their windows and enjoy the scene. You can see the stunning 20 atolls with their vibrant colors, it is a mixture of blue, green, emerald and turquoise with very white sandy beaches. We were so jealous of the passengers who were going to disembark in Maldives.

In the Capital City Male

Our airline had some schedule and route changes and decided that the crew would have layovers in Maldives, and of course we were over the moon when we heard about that. Everyone started bidding and requesting for that flight, so it was really hard to get scheduled for it in the beginning. You were considered a very lucky crew member if someone called sick from the Maldives operating crew, and they pulled you from standby to do that flight.

I got scheduled for that flight a few times enough to taste the spirit of Maldives and get an idea of what it is about. Of course, my experience is different from the tourists who go there and spend more time. The maximum I spent there was 48 hours, and I still got to do many things in them. When we landed at the airport, we observed that Maldives customs officials are very strict. Importation of alcohol is strictly prohibited and they often search your bags, and if they find any, they confiscate them and issue a fine. You can still buy alcohol though once you get into the country, you just cannot bring it from abroad. We arrived in the capital city of Maldives which is called Male, and our hotel was located there. The city has a big port where ferries go back and forth all the day, and famous resorts send their private boats to transport passengers who booked with them to their location. Some passengers also get transported to their atolls via helicopter trips.

People who have the situation like us and happen to be staying in Male can arrange trips in one of the resorts to spend a full day there or to stay overnight. You can either book it through the hotel or contact the resorts and they can pick you up from the port. At that point I got to know that actually people who are on budgets can still go to Maldives and enjoy, as there are different options of resorts and hotel packages that fit people with economic budgets. When we arrived, it was almost 3pm which was too late to go to resorts, and we postponed that till next morning, so we arranged and booked everything that afternoon we landed. At night time, we spent the time in the city of Male, which has the local people, schools and hospitals, and we got to try some Maldivian dishes there.

There was a communication problem there in the city as hardly anyone spoke English, and the local people were very reserved and quiet. They do not mingle with the tourists that much; however they are not rude or impolite. They just keep their distance and they will do their best to help you if you need it even if there is a language problem. I personally did not like the local restaurant experience as the food was kind of different for me, and I consider myself a picky eater. I concluded that I do not like the Maldivian cuisine after trying food in the capital city Male, and also ordering some food from the hotel we stayed at, as well as the food in the resort we booked for the next day. I also entered a convenience store to buy some snacks such as chips and chocolates and I did not like them at all. The only thing that I really liked there is fresh tropical fruits, they were really delicious. They also sell some imported products but they are way too expensive, and the tap water there is desalinated and safe to drink but it does not taste that good. I had to buy bottled mineral water which was also very expensive. We were provided with two complementary water bottles in the hotel rooms, however I had to buy more and they were costly.

Maldives has tropical warm weather all year, and the best season to visit Maldives is between November and April, as it is away from the monsoon season. We were warned by the hotel about our trip the next day, that in case the weather gets bad we might be stuck on the island that has the resort. I heard that some flight attendants of some airlines got fired because they went to a resort, and could not come back before their scheduled flight, as they were stuck on an island, which caused a very bad reputation for the airline. Imagine passengers would go to the airport ready for the flights and the crew to operate the flight were absent and stuck on an island

Embudu Village Resort

Whether you decide to stay at the hotel in the capital city of Male or decide to explore other resorts, you would still have a fabulous time. Most of the hotels in Male have sea-water swimming pools as most of the hotels are very close to the port, and they have normal swimming pools as well. In the evenings, you can have a chilled time at the hotel buffet or bar. Nightlife in Maldives is not that popular as the destination itself is for couples to enjoy a quiet and a private time, and also the local people are mostly conservative religious muslims and the majority of them do not drink alcohol or party at night. There are nightclubs and bars of course, however they are not the main focus of attraction to people in Maldives.

As I mentioned above, I visited Maldives a few times. Some of these times I would just chill in the hotel, and two of these times I decided to go and explore two different rosters, which were matching my budget with my colleagues. The first time we visited the Embudu Village Resort for a day use, and the second time we went to Meeru Island Resort and Spa for an overnight stay.

We took the ferry early in the morning to Embudu Village Resort that included access to the beach, swimming and snorkeling, and a full buffet at lunch. We started by exploring the resort and relaxing on the beach for sometime, while some of us went snorkeling. We were taking pictures of the amazing view, the seal gulls on the beach, the colorful fish that were swimming in the shallow crystal-clear water and the tall coconut trees. We hardly felt the presence of local people, however if you ever needed them, they suddenly showed up which was fantastic. They are considered there and not there at the same time, and they are fully aware of what is happening in the location, however they will never bother the tourists. This gives the tourists a feeling of more privacy and freedom, especially the honeymooners.

At lunch time, we got to meet the other tourists who were spending nights in the resort, as well as the ones on day trips like us. Lunch was kind of a social gathering in an open air buffet on the beach, and all the servers were local people who maintained the discreet and understated interaction. As I explained above, my personal experience with the Maldivian cuisine was not that great, however to some other people it might be the opposite. I tried some dishes such as fish curry and Garudhiya which is a local fish soup, however I ended up going to the dessert section where I ate a lot of tropical fruits such as papaya, passion fruit, pineapple and guava.

Towards the end of the afternoon, we started packing our belongings to take the ferry back to the hotel, and the weather started to get a bit gloomy and the skies became a bit gray. We were advised to wait for the ferry on time because of the unpredictable weather conditions so that we do not miss it.

One more thing I would like to highlight is the hot sun of Maldives. Maldives sits on the equator with extremely high UV rays, people get burned very quickly when they just walk for a few minutes under the sun, or they go swimming. It is extremely important to pack high quality sunscreens for that trip.

Meeru Island Resort and Spa

The Meeru Island resort experience was kind of different than the Embudu Village one, as it was a bigger resort and had a lot of varieties and activities to do, we also got to have a sense of spending the night time in Maldives there. I would name the Meeru resort as a kind of a family or friendsâ€™ resort where you can play tennis, golf, volleyball on the beach, take boat excursion trips and bicycle rides. There is a big fitness center, and billiard game room if you are not a fan of spending time under the sun or swimming. There is also the Meeru Museum where it tells the history of the start of Maldives Tourism industry and the role that Meeru island played in that.

The night life at the Meeru Resort is quite vibrant too, there is a nightclub and Maldivian cultural night shows on the beach. The difference between our stay in this resort and the Embudu Village day use one is the night time Bodhu Beru dance and drum show. It is a heritage tradition on the beach while people are eating dinner and they invite you to dance and play the drums with them under the moonlight. My colleagues and I were glad that we did not have to take the ferry at the end of the day like my last trip, and that we had the whole night to enjoy the Maldives.

The local people in this resort are more involved with the tourists as there are more visitors there, and lots of families with kids, which requires more attention and interaction from the local people's side in comparison to the quiet and private resorts.

Islandness in Maldives

The people of Maldives are very proud of their culture, and you can sense that strongly when they interact with tourists. They keep their own language, they are always together and they love their island the most. The Maldives Independence Day is a very significant event there that shows the love of locals to their island. It is a big celebration event there with lots of cultural and heritage shows. They are also very reserved and they enforce their cultural habits when they want to. I remember it happened once when I had Maldives flight during the holy month of Ramadan where some restaurants were closed during the daylight hours, as people were observing Ramadan and fasting, however they are more flexible in private resorts and hotels. They often advise tourists before going to Maldives to make sure that their trip is not falling during the month of Ramadan in case some activities are temporarily stopped because of that.

Being an island, you get to experience vulnerability and isolation in Maldives. This happens when sudden cancellations of ferries or flights occur due to weather conditions. You get to feel that as well when the monsoon season attacks the island. Maldives had a history of Tsunami in 2004 that caused major damage and a high number of deaths. Islanders there for sure know and experienced the meanings of vulnerability, resilience, togetherness and independence as they passed through all these events in their history.
